A Note on the Technical Pre-requisites
It is necessary to acknowledge that the script relies on a pre-existing installation of rclone, an open-source command-line program used to manage files on cloud storage. Before one can generate a manifest, this utility must be configured to communicate with your specific Google Drive or OneDrive account. This step is a primary requirement; without a successful connection, the indexer will be unable to retrieve the file paths or snippets required for the manifest.
The setup involves a one-time configuration process where you grant the utility permission to access your cloud storage through a secure authentication flow. I am hopeful that the following references will provide a plain and steady guide for those who are new to this tool. While the initial setup may seem technical, it is a stable foundation that allows for the automated organization of one's digital life. I trust that these resources will be of some small assistance in establishing your local environment.
Guidance for rclone Configuration
For those seeking to establish this connection, the following documentation provides the necessary steps for various cloud providers:
•	Official Installation Guide: This resource covers the installation of the utility across different operating systems, including Windows, macOS, and Linux. rclone.org/install
•	Google Drive Setup: A declarative guide on creating a "remote" connection, including the authentication steps required by Google. rclone.org/drive
•	Microsoft OneDrive Setup: Detailed instructions for connecting to personal or business OneDrive accounts. rclone.org/onedrive
•	The rclone config Command: This is the primary interactive command used to set up a new remote connection by following a series of plain prompts.
I have focused on these specific guides because they are maintained by the creators of the tool and remain the most accurate source of information. Once your "remote" is named and tested—for instance, as gdrive: or onedrive:—you may then update the configuration in the script to match this name. I am hopeful that once this initial hurdle is cleared, the rest of the indexing process will proceed without further complication.
